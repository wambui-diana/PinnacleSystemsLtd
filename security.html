Security Sector

It refers to a means or method by which something is secured through a system of interworking components and devices. Security systems differ from each other in terms of purpose, type and the device used to give a certain level of protection.

Purchasing a security solution is not just a one-off acquisition but rather a significant long term investment where relationships need to be built. By choosing an integrated building security system you have peace of mind that your expectations will be met and we will continue to understand and react to your needs throughout the lifetime of the system.

This includes three main categories:

a) Alarm Systems

A car alarm is an electronic device installed in a vehicle in an attempt to discourage theft of the vehicle itself, its contents, or both. Car alarms work by emitting high-volume sound (often a vehicle-mounted siren, klaxon, pre-recorded verbal warning, the vehicle's own horn, or a combination of these) when the conditions necessary for triggering it are met.


b) Biometric Systems

These are systems that provide a form of identification and access control within an organization. They employ the use of biometric identifiers which are the distinctive and measurable characteristics used to label and describe individuals.


c) CCTV Systems

They involve a network of cameras with a central recording hub to provide security from intruders and can be used in either residential, commercial or industrial settings.
